'Dress me slowly, I'm in a hurry'

or why slow fashion matters?

We live in a fast world. Fast food, fast cars...fast fashion. We always rush and yet feel like there is not enough time to do it all. The solution is simpler than you might think - to slow down.


The fashion world is no different. It is just as hurried and frenzied. The need to start behaving more ethically to the nature and workers behind the trendy pieces in our closets is alarming - the need for slow fashion.


Slow fashion, you guessed it correctly, is the negation to fast fashion. This sustainable concept focuses on 'slowing down' the processes in the industry. Such brands release fewer collections, which reflects in the substantial decrease in waste production, increase in the quality, and improvement of the working conditions. Slow fashion aims to preserve the craft and environment and, most importantly, provide value.


Buena Onda, one of the slow-fashion pioneers, created a timeless business model, ‘The simplified retail model’. Its essence lies in releasing only three items every summer, with each collection connecting to the next, making ultra-slow, ultra-cool! The brand’s mindset could be summarized in a saying: ‘Dress me slowly, I’m in a hurry’. Even though there are speculations about the author of this quote, whoever it was, he couldn't have said it better.


So buena onda and sin prisa, folks!

Buena Onda’s advice on how to become a 'slow-fashionista'

01

Choose a brand wisely

Go for the right fashion brands - ethical, sustainable and transparent about what they do. Brands are just like people - the ones, who have nothing to hide, are usually the ones you can trust.

02

Look out for fabrics

Prioritize organic, sustainable fabrics such as cotton, linen or wool. Do yourself and the environment a favour and avoid fabrics made with the use of chemicals.

03

Read the labels

..and follow the washing instructions is not fun advice. But it could seriously save your clothes from early retirement.

04

Go for quality over quantity

No brainer. You’ll see that the investment in high-quality fashion will pay off in the long run.

05

Slow down with the washing

Try to collect your clothes until you fill the entire machine first. It will save energy and protect the fabrics.

06

Don’t end the clothes’ life prematurely

Did you get tired of those shorts? Get crafty with them, swap them, sell them, donate them… There are just so many ways.

07

Zoom in on the timeless pieces

And lastly, don’t try to desperately keep up with the latest trends. Set your own with a few clothing gems, and you will never get out of style.
